Woodworking or Problem Solving with Wood

Oak End Tables

I received new appreciation for the hard in hardwood when I made the mortisse and tenon joints for this oak table. I started out using a hand chisel.

I did some research and used dowels on the second one in the set. It's still strong enough to sit on.

Plant Table

To help this plant table handle a water spill a penny top was added. About $7 in pennies did the job.

Candle Holders

This is re-purposed wood, but it didn't look its age. To get the aged look it was stained black, sanded and stained again with Golden Oak.

Picture Frame

The inner frames were the failed part of another project so the outer frame was added to cover the damage and add color.

Wooden Vase

The octagon shape requires beveled cuts on a table saw and with such small pieces it takes some planning to make sure my fingers stay attached. Epoxy resin on the inside means it can hold water.
